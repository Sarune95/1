What is Agile?

Agile is an approach to project management and software development that gives flexibility, collaboration and continuous improvement. Instead of following a plan such as waterfall methodology from start to finish, Agile teams work in short iterations called sprints to deliver small parts of a product.

12 Key principles of Agile

These principles provide a framework for Agile teams to work effectively, prioritize customer satisfaction and respond to change in a dynamic and collaborative manner.

1. Customer satisfaction through the whole process: Delivering valuable software very frequently in a short time frame.

2. Adapt fast to changing Requirements: Agile processes change for the customer competitive advantage.

3. Frequent delivery of working software: from a couple of weeks to a couple of months with a short timescale.

4. Collaborating between business stakeholders and developers: they must work together daily throught the project.

5. Trusting and supportive of each other environment: give them the environment with motivation, support they need and trust them to get the job done.

6. Face to face conversations: this is the most effective method of conveying information and within a development team.

7. Working software as the primary measure of progress: working software is the primary measure of progress.

8. Sustainable development pace: Agile methodology processes promote sustainable development. The sponsors, developers and users should be bale to maintain a constant pace.

9. Technical excelellence experience and good design: continuous attention to technical excellence and good design enchances agility.

10. Simplicity:maximizing the amount of work not done is essential.

11. Self organized teams: best architectures, requirements and designs emerge from self organized teams.

12. Reflection and adaptation: at regular intervals, the team reflects on how to become more effective, then tunes and adjusts its behavior accordingly.

Advantages of Agile

More control: Work can be broken down into parts and conducted in rapid, iterative cycles. The regular meetings that are part of agile allow project teams to share progress, discuss problems and work it out the solutions. It makes the entire process more transparent.

Better productivity: Projects are completed in shorter sprints, making them more manageable. It also allows products to be rolled out faster and changes to be easily made at any point during the process.

Better quality: ability to find problems and create solutions quickly and efficiently. Flexibility of the agile method allows project teams to respond to customer reaction and constantly to improve the product.

Higher customer satisfaction: collaborating closely between the project team and the customer it provides immediate feedback. Team is able to make tweaks for the customer to their expectations and desires throught the process. This leaves the customer more satisfied.

Higher return on investment: the end product is ready for market faster, staying ahead of the competition and quickly repeating benefits. The benefits are to cut cost and time to market in half, while increasing application quality and customer satisfaction.
