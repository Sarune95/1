Waterfall methodology

Waterfall method is an approach to a sequential project management where tasks are completed one after another. It follows the steps of:

  • Requirement gathering
  • Design
  • Implementation
  • Testing/Deployment
  • Maintenance

Each stage is completed before moving to the next, making it hard to backtrack if changes needed later.

Requirement gathering

The requirement phase states what the system should do. At this stage you determine the projects scope from business obligation to user needs.

Design

Once all requirements are collected, the focus shifts to the design phase. At the design phase designers work on crafting solutions aligned with the gathering requirements.

Implementation

Once the design undergoes finalization and receives approval, the implementation phase commences. Design teams pass on their specifications to developers for execution. This stage also unveils any potential implementation challenges. If significant obstacles arise, reverting to the design phase for reassessment becomes necessary.

Testing/Deployment

Once the developers finish writing the code based on the design, its time for quality assurance. This means checking everything to make sure the product works well for users.

Maintenance

After the product is launched, developers might need to fix any bugs that appear. Customers report any problems they encounter to your support team. Then its the teams job to deal with those reports and release updated versions of the product. Each stage relies on the one before and thats why there is not much room for mistakes between or within these phases.

Lets say if a stakeholder wants to add a new requirement during the verification phase, you will need to review the entire project again. This could lead to scrapping everything and starting from the scrach again.

Requirement gathering

Identify resources: Determine the resources needed for the project such as manpower, tools and budget allocation

Assign team roles: Define the responsibilities of each team member and assign tasks according to their expertise and the projects needs.

Create project timeline: Develop a timeline outlining the duration of each stage of the project ensuring deadlines are set for completion.

Design

Establish schedules: Set up schedules and project milestones to ensure progress is made in a timely manner, goals are met within the designated timeframe.

Define deliverables: Specify the exact deliverables exected from the project, whether they are software components or physical products, ensuring clarity in expectations.

Develop designs: Create designs or blueprints for the deliverables, outlining the structure, functionality and visual aspects of the final product.

Implementation

Formulate Implementation Strategy: Develop a strategic plan for implementing the designed solutions, considering factors such as workflow, resource allocation, and potential challenges.

Gather data and research: Collect necessary data or conduct research essential for the build phase, ensuring informed decision-making and effective problem-solving.

Task delegation: Assign specific tasks to team members based on their skills and expertise, ensuring efficient distribution of responsibilities and optimal utilization of resources.

Testing/Deployment

Write test cases: Testers create comprehensive test cases outlining what aspects of the product will be tested to ensure functionality and reliability.

Track issues: Keep track of any issues or bugs identified during testing, documenting them for resolution and future reference.

Sequential testing: Conduct testing one element at a time to isolate and address potential issues systematically, ensuring thorough evaluation of the product's performance.

Define quality metrics: Determine the criteria and metrics used to evaluate the product's quality and performance, establishing benchmarks for success.

Scenario testing: Test the product under various scenarios and conditions to simulate real world usage and identify any potential usability issues or shortcomings.

Maintenance

Bug fixes: Address any bugs or issues that arise postlaunch promptly, ensuring the product maintains optimal functionality and user experience.

Customer support: Provide support to customers encountering problems with the product, responding to inquiries and troubleshooting issues effectively.

Release updates: Release updated versions of the product containing bug fixes, improvements, or new features based on user feedback and evolving requirements.
